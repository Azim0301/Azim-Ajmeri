Jalebi Recipe

  1. Ingredients:
    • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
    • 1 tablespoon chickpea flour (besan)
    • 1 cup yogurt (dahi)
    • 1 cup sugar
    • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder (optional, for color)
    • 1/2 cup water
    • Ghee or oil for frying
  2. Instructions:
    1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, chickpea flour, yogurt, and water. Mix well to form a smooth batter. Cover and let it ferment for 12-24 hours.
    2. After fermentation, add baking powder and turmeric powder to the batter. Mix well.
    3. Heat ghee or oil in a deep frying pan.
    4. Pour the batter into a piping bag or a squeeze bottle with a small hole nozzle.
    5. Squeeze the batter into the hot oil in circular motions to form jalebi shapes.
    6. Fry until the jalebis are crisp and golden brown.
    7. Meanwhile, prepare sugar syrup by dissolving sugar in water over medium heat until it reaches one-string consistency.
    8. Dip the fried jalebis in the sugar syrup for a few seconds, then remove and place them on a plate to drain excess syrup.
    9. Serve hot and enjoy!

Dabeli is a popular Indian street food originating from the Kutch region of Gujarat. It's a type of spicy snack made by mixing boiled potatoes with a special blend of spices known as dabeli masala. This mixture is then stuffed into a pav (soft bread roll) along with various chutneys, such as tamarind and garlic, along with crunchy peanuts and pomegranate seeds for texture and sweetness. It's typically garnished with sev (crunchy chickpea noodles) and served hot.
